# Veteran Injury Tracker — How to Use

A step-by-step guide to documenting your VA disability claim.

---

## Getting Started

### On a Computer
1. Open `ENDEX (Standalone).html` in any web browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari).
2. Double-click the file — it opens directly. No internet required after loading (except for fonts).

### On Your Phone (iPhone or Android)

**Option A — Transfer the file:**
1. Send `ENDEX (Standalone).html` to your phone (email it to yourself, AirDrop, Google Drive, iCloud, USB, etc.).
2. Open the file from your email or file manager — it will open in your phone's browser.
3. **iPhone tip:** If it won't open from Files, tap Share > "Open in Safari."
4. **Android tip:** Open it from Downloads or Files — it should launch in Chrome automatically.

**Option B — Host it (for easy access):**
1. Upload the file to a free hosting service or your own Google Drive.
2. For Google Drive: upload the file > right-click > Share > "Anyone with the link" > copy link.
3. Open that link on your phone's browser.

**Add to Home Screen (acts like an app):**
- **iPhone:** Open in Safari > tap the Share button (square with arrow) > "Add to Home Screen."
- **Android:** Open in Chrome > tap the three-dot menu > "Add to home screen."
- Now you can launch it like a regular app from your phone.

## Step 1: Select Your Condition (Primary Map Tab)

This is where you document what's wrong and where.

### Using Quick Select (Recommended)
1. Look at the **Quick Select** panel on the right side (or below on phone).
2. **Search:** Type in the search bar to find a specific condition (e.g., "tinnitus," "knee," "PTSD"). Results show the condition name and which body area it belongs to.
3. **Browse:** Or scroll through the body area groups (Mental Health, Neck/Shoulders, Legs, etc.).
4. Tap a body area to open its evaluation panel.

### Using the Body Map
1. Choose **Front** or **Back** view and **Male** or **Female** body.
2. Click/tap directly on the body image where your injury is.
3. A pin drops and the injury form opens.
4. Fill in: date, what happened, where you were stationed, severity, and any functional impacts.
5. Hit **Save Injury** — the pin stays on the map.

### Evaluation Panels (Knee, Back, Shoulder, Mental Health, etc.)
1. When you select a body area like "Knees" or "Mental Health," a dedicated evaluation panel opens.
2. **Search or scroll** through the condition list to find yours.
3. **Select one condition** (physical areas are single-select; mental health allows multiple).
4. Choose your side if applicable (Left, Right, or Both — bilateral claims get a purple pin).
5. Rate each domain using the VA criteria shown.
6. When done, click **Place Pin on Map** to mark it on the body diagram.

### After Placing a Pin
A toast message appears at the bottom: **"Pin placed! Add secondaries next."**
Click the button to jump straight to the Secondary tab, or dismiss it and keep adding more conditions.

---

## Step 2: Rate Severity & Add Secondary Conditions

Click the **Secondary** tab.

### Rating Your Condition
1. Each condition you added appears as a card.
2. Click **"Rate"** to open the rating questionnaire.
3. Answer the questions based on your experience — the app calculates a suggested VA percentage.

### Adding Secondary Conditions
Secondary conditions are problems *caused by* your primary condition (e.g., knee injury caused back pain, PTSD caused insomnia).

1. Under each primary condition, look for **"Veterans commonly also claim"** — these are suggestion chips showing the most common secondary claims for that condition, with official VA diagnostic codes.
2. **Click a chip** to instantly add that secondary.
3. Or use the dropdown/custom input to add something not listed.
4. Rate each secondary the same way.

---

## Step 3: Special Claims

Click the **Special Claims** tab for:

- **Vocational Conditions** — How your disabilities affect your ability to work.
- **SMC (Special Monthly Compensation)** — Extra payments for loss of body parts, needing daily help, etc. These are flat dollar amounts on top of your regular disability check.
- **Presumptive Service Connection** — If you served in certain places/times (Vietnam, Gulf War, burn pits), the VA automatically assumes your conditions are service-connected. Check any that apply and fill in your service details.
- **Physical Loss & Paired Organs** — Special rules for lost limbs or when both sides of a paired organ are affected.
- **MST (Military Sexual Trauma)** — Conditions caused by MST are rated under mental health. The MST section shows suggestion chips for common conditions, has a Privacy Shield to hide details throughout the app, and tracks your evidence. The VA has relaxed evidence rules for MST claims.

---

## Step 4: Review Your Timeline

Click the **Timeline** tab.

- See all your conditions in chronological order.
- Each entry shows the date, details, severity, and any missing evidence.
- Use this to verify everything is complete before exporting.

---

## Step 5: Check Your Rating Estimate

Click the **Rating** tab.

- See your estimated combined VA disability percentage.
- The VA uses "VA Math" — each rating is applied to the remaining healthy percentage, not simply added together.
- **Mental health** follows the single rating rule: all MH conditions (including MST-caused ones) get one combined rating.
- **Bilateral conditions** (affecting both sides) get a 10% bonus.
- You can override any rating by typing a new percentage.

---

## Step 6: Write Your Personal Statement

Click the **Notes & Statement** tab.

- Use the text editor to write your statement in your own words.
- Explain how your conditions started, how they've gotten worse, and how they affect daily life.
- The right sidebar lists all your logged conditions for quick reference — click any to insert the name into your statement.

---

## Step 7: Export Your Records

Click the **Export** button in the top-right corner:

- **PDF / Print Summary** — Opens a formatted page with body diagrams, pin locations, numbered conditions, evidence gaps, detailed cards for each injury, evaluation results, rating breakdown, and your personal statement. Use Ctrl+P (or Cmd+P on Mac) to save as PDF.
- **CSV Spreadsheet** — Downloads a spreadsheet you can open in Excel or Google Sheets.
- **TXT Report** — Plain text file.

### On your phone:
- For PDF: tap "PDF / Print Summary" > it opens in a new tab > tap Share > "Print" or "Save as PDF."
- For CSV/TXT: the file downloads to your phone's Downloads folder.

---

## Tips

- **Pins are draggable.** Click and drag any pin to move it to a more accurate position.
- **Click a pin** to edit the injury details or open its evaluation panel.
- **Evidence gaps** are flagged automatically — look for orange badges showing which fields are missing.
- **Import/Export:** You can export to CSV and later import it back to restore your data.
- **All data stays on your device.** Nothing is sent to any server. If you clear your browser, the data is gone — export regularly.
- **The disclaimer at the bottom is important:** This is a documentation tool, not a VA decision engine. Always talk to a VSO or VA-accredited representative before filing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need internet to use this?**
A: No. The standalone file works completely offline. The only thing that needs internet is the Google Fonts (Teko, Rajdhani, Oswald, and Open Sans) — if offline, your browser will use fallback fonts and everything still works.

**Q: Will my data be saved if I close the browser?**
A: No — data lives only in your current browser session. Use **Save Project** (encrypted .endexclaim file) before closing, then **Load Project** to restore EVERYTHING — evaluations, ratings, special claims, and your statement. CSV export only covers basic injury rows, and PDF cannot be re-imported at all.

**Q: Can I use this on my phone?**
A: Yes. The app is responsive and adapts to phone screens. See "Getting Started" above for how to open it on your phone and add it to your home screen.

**Q: What's the difference between primary and secondary conditions?**
A: A primary condition is directly caused by military service. A secondary condition is caused or worsened by a primary condition. For example: a service-connected knee injury (primary) that caused chronic back pain (secondary).

**Q: Why does mental health only give one rating?**
A: The VA rates all mental health conditions under one combined rating — your highest evaluated condition sets the rate. Even if you have PTSD, depression, and anxiety, they all count as one mental health rating.

**Q: What does the purple pin mean?**
A: A purple pin indicates a bilateral condition — one that affects both sides of your body (e.g., both knees). Bilateral conditions get a 10% bonus in the VA rating calculation.
