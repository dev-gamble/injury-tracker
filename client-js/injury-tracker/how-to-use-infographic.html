Quick Start Guide

How to Use ENDEX

Index your injuries. Build stronger VA claims. This guide walks you through every step — from logging your first condition to exporting your complete claim package.

1
Select Your Condition

Search or browse the Quick Select panel on the right side. Type a condition name (like "tinnitus" or "knee") to search across all body areas, or browse by category.

Click a body area (Knees, Shoulder, Mental Health, etc.) to open its evaluation panel. You'll pick one condition at a time, choose your side (Left / Right / Both), then rate it using VA diagnostic criteria.

Custom pins: Click directly on the body image to drop a pin anywhere for conditions not covered by the standard panels.

💡 Did you know? Mental health is the only area where you can select multiple conditions at once. The VA rates all mental health conditions under one combined rating — so PTSD, depression, and anxiety together still get one number.

2
Rate Severity & Add Secondaries

After placing your pin, go to the Severity & Secondary tab. Each condition shows a rating questionnaire based on real VA diagnostic criteria.

"Veterans commonly also claim" — suggestion chips appear showing the most frequently filed secondary conditions for your primary, with official VA diagnostic codes (DC codes). Click one to add it instantly.

A secondary condition is something caused or made worse by your service-connected condition — not something that happened separately.

💡 Did you know? Secondary claims are where most veterans leave money on the table. A 10% tinnitus rating can open the door to secondary claims for migraines, anxiety, and depression — each with its own rating that adds to your total.

3
Understand VA Math

The VA does not simply add your ratings together. They use a formula that applies each rating to the remaining healthy percentage of your body.

Condition 1: 50% → 100 - 50 = 50% remaining
Condition 2: 30% of 50 = 15 → 50 - 15 = 35% remaining
Condition 3: 10% of 35 = 3.5 → 35 - 3.5 = 31.5% remaining
Total disability: 100 - 31.5 = 68.5% → Rounded to 70%

50 + 30 + 10 = 90% with regular math, but only 70% with VA math. The Rating tab calculates this automatically.

💡 Did you know? Bilateral conditions (same problem on both sides) get a 10% bonus in the calculation. If both knees are rated 10%, the VA treats them as 19% combined instead of 10% each. This app automatically detects bilateral conditions.

4
Special Claims & Evidence

The Special Claims tab covers entitlements many veterans don't know about:

⚖ Presumptive Claims

Served near burn pits, Agent Orange, or in the Gulf? The VA automatically assumes your conditions are service-connected. No nexus letter needed.

🔒 MST Claims

You do NOT need a police report. The VA accepts buddy statements, behavior changes, and counseling records. Privacy Shield hides details throughout the app.

💰 SMC Payments

Extra monthly payments on top of your rating — for loss of body parts, needing daily help, or being housebound. Even 0% rated vets can get SMC-K.

⚠ Evidence Gaps

The app automatically flags incomplete records. Green = complete. Orange = missing fields. Fix gaps before filing to strengthen your claim.

💡 Did you know? The PACT Act (2022) expanded burn pit and toxic exposure coverage dramatically. If you served post-9/11 near burn pits, you may be eligible even without a current diagnosis — enroll in VA healthcare now to establish a record.
5
Write, Review & Export

Personal Statement — Write your story in the built-in editor. A sidebar lists all your conditions for quick reference — click any to insert the name. This is also where you document how conditions affect your ability to work (vocational impact).

Timeline — See all your conditions in chronological order. Verify dates, check for gaps, and make sure nothing was missed.

Export — Generate a PDF/Print summary with body diagrams, numbered pins, evidence gaps, and detailed condition cards. Also available as CSV or TXT.

Quick Tips Veterans Wish They Knew
★ Claim Everything
If it happened in service or because of service, claim it. Even a 0% rating establishes service connection — you can increase it later if it gets worse.
★ Worst Day, Not Best Day
At your C&P exam, describe your WORST days — not your best. The VA rates based on functional impairment at its peak, including flare-ups.
★ Buddy Statements Are Powerful
A fellow service member, spouse, or friend who witnessed your condition or its effects can write a statement. This is often the evidence that tips a claim from denied to approved.
★ Don't Wait to File
File within one year of discharge and your rating is backdated to Day 1. After that, it starts from the date you file. File an Intent to File (ITF) immediately to lock in your effective date — you get 1 year to submit the full claim.
★ Save Your Data
This app runs entirely in your browser. Nothing is saved on any server. Use the Import/Export button to save your project file — if you close the browser without saving, your data is gone.
★ Free Help Exists
Veterans Service Organizations (DAV, VFW, American Legion) will help you file for free. VA-accredited attorneys only get paid if you win. You don't have to do this alone.
