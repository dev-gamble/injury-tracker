1
Place Your Primary Pins
Tab 1 — Primary Map

Start with the Quick Select sidebar. Search for a condition (like "tinnitus" or "knee") or browse body areas. Toggle the body diagram between Front and Back views using the buttons above the image.

Pick one condition at a time. Click a body area (Knee, Shoulder, Back, Mental Health, etc.) to open its evaluation panel. Rate severity using real VA diagnostic criteria from 38 CFR Part 4, then place the pin on the body.

Side prompt — Left, Right, or Both. For paired areas, ENDEX asks which side. If you choose Both, it's saved as bilateral — same condition, same rating on each side — so the VA bilateral factor can apply later.

Custom pins — click directly on the body anywhere to drop a pin for conditions not covered by a standard panel.

Name / ID field at the top of this tab adds your name or case number to every export so reviewers know whose file they're reading.

💡 Did you know? Mental health is the one area where you can select multiple conditions at once. The VA rates all mental health conditions under a single combined rating — PTSD, depression, and anxiety together still produce one number.

2
Rate Severity & Add Secondaries
Tab 2 — Severity & Secondary

Every primary you pinned shows up here with a severity questionnaire built from real VA criteria. Answer the questions and ENDEX surfaces the rating percentage the VA typically assigns for that level of impairment.

"Veterans commonly also claim" — for each primary, ENDEX shows suggestion chips of the secondary conditions most frequently filed with it, each tagged with its DC code (38 CFR Part 4). Click a chip to add it instantly.

A secondary condition is something caused or made worse by a service-connected condition — not a separate injury. Example: service-connected knee → hip pain from altered gait → lower back pain.

Evidence gaps are flagged automatically when a condition is missing key fields (date, description, side). Fix gaps before exporting to strengthen your claim.

💡 Did you know? Secondaries are the most commonly missed part of a claim. A single primary can support several secondaries — a service-connected knee may tie to hip, back, or gait issues; tinnitus often ties to migraines, anxiety, depression, and sleep problems. Each is tracked separately so nothing falls through the cracks.
⚠ Pyramiding check. When you export, ENDEX runs a 38 CFR 4.14 pyramiding / duplicate review and flags conditions that may overlap — rating the same impairment twice under different codes. A flag isn't proof of pyramiding; bring it to your VSO or attorney.

3
Handle Special Claims
Tab 3 — Special Claims

The Special Claims tab covers four categories with unique VA rules — entitlements many veterans don't know to ask about.

⚖ Presumptive

Burn pits, Agent Orange, Gulf War, radiation exposure. The VA automatically assumes service connection — no nexus letter required when your service matches a qualifying area and time period.

🔒 MST

Military Sexual Trauma. No police report needed. The VA accepts buddy statements, counseling records, and behavioral changes as evidence. A privacy shield hides MST details throughout the app.

💰 SMC

Special Monthly Compensation. Extra payments on top of your rating for loss of a limb, loss of use, aid-and-attendance, or being housebound. Even 0% rated vets can qualify for SMC-K.

⚠ Physical Loss

Loss of limb, organ, or creative organ. Documented separately because it triggers automatic SMC entitlements and can affect combined-rating math in the veteran's favor.

💡 Did you know? The PACT Act (2022) expanded burn pit and toxic exposure coverage dramatically. If you served post-9/11 near burn pits, you may be presumptively eligible even without a current diagnosis — enrolling in VA healthcare now establishes the record.
4
Review Your Timeline
Tab 4 — Timeline

The Timeline is a chronological view of every condition you've logged, ordered by date. Use it to sanity-check your claim before you file.

Items only appear when they have a date. If a condition is missing from the timeline, open it back on the Primary Map and fill in when it started (or when you were first diagnosed / treated).

Look for: gaps where something should exist, clusters of conditions tied to one event or deployment, and missing evidence for older entries where records may need to be requested.

Why it matters: The VA looks for a clear narrative from in-service event → current disability. A clean timeline makes that narrative obvious to the rater and to anyone helping you file.
5
Understand Your Rating
Tab 5 — Rating

The VA does not simply add your ratings together. It uses a formula that applies each rating to the remaining healthy percentage of your body, then rounds to the nearest 10%. ENDEX does this math automatically.

Condition 1: 50% → 100 − 50 = 50% remaining
Condition 2: 30% of 50 = 15 → 50 − 15 = 35% remaining
Condition 3: 10% of 35 = 3.5 → 35 − 3.5 = 31.5% remaining
Total: 100 − 31.5 = 68.5% → Rounded to 70%

Regular math: 50 + 30 + 10 = 90%. VA math: 70%. This is why combining small ratings matters so much — they stack less as you go.

💡 Bilateral factor. When the same condition is claimed on both sides (both knees, both shoulders, both hands), the VA adds a 10% bilateral factor to that pair before combining with the rest. ENDEX detects bilateral pins automatically and shows the factor on the Rating tab.
6
Capture Notes & Export
Tab 6 — Notes & Statement

Notes & Statement is a flexible workspace for everything that doesn't fit neatly on the other tabs. Use it however is most useful to you:

• Draft a VA personal statement in your own words
• Log doctor visits, provider names, dates, and phone calls with the VA
• Keep questions and reminders for your VSO or attorney
• Capture context on specific conditions — what triggers flare-ups, what helps, who witnessed events
• Paste research, links, or anything else you want travelling with this file

A sidebar on the right lists every condition you've logged — click any entry to drop its name into your notes so you don't have to retype it.

Write for whoever reads this next — a VSO, an attorney, a family member helping, or yourself in six months. Include the details that won't show up elsewhere: dates, places, names, how symptoms affect your routine, and what you've already tried. Specifics make everything downstream easier.

Export when you're done. Use the Import / Export button in the header. Nothing is saved automatically — always export before closing.

Quick Tips Veterans Wish They Knew
★ Claim Everything
If it happened in service or because of service, claim it. Even a 0% rating establishes service connection — you can increase it later if the condition gets worse.
★ Worst Day, Not Best Day
At your C&P exam, describe your worst days, not your best. The VA rates based on functional impairment at its peak, including flare-ups.
★ Buddy Statements Are Powerful
A fellow service member, spouse, or friend who witnessed your condition or its effects can write a statement. This is often the evidence that flips a claim from denied to approved.
★ File Intent to File Immediately
File within one year of discharge and your effective date is backdated to Day 1. Afterward, file an ITF to lock in today's date — then you get a full year to submit the complete claim.
★ Export Before You Close
ENDEX runs entirely in your browser. Nothing is saved to any server. Use Save Project to download a .vaclaim file — it's the only way to pick up where you left off.
★ Free Help Exists
Veterans Service Organizations (DAV, VFW, American Legion) will help you file for free. VA-accredited attorneys only get paid if you win. You don't have to do this alone.
